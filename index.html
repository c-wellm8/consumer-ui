Sage · why this matters
Your afternoon crashes aren't about willpower — they're about blood sugar spikes and drops. A high-carb snack at 2pm causes a spike, then a sharp drop by 3–4pm. That drop is what's stealing your evenings.
Why skip the afternoon snack?
📉
The spike-crash cycle
High-carb snacks cause blood sugar to rise quickly, then fall sharply — triggering fatigue and brain fog within 90 minutes.
🕒
Your prediabetes context
With HbA1c at 6.2%, your body is slower to clear glucose. The afternoon window is when this is most disruptive to your energy.
Smart snacks if you need one
🥑
Handful of nuts + apple slices
Healthy fat and fiber slow glucose absorption — energy stays stable without a spike.
🥚
Hard-boiled egg + cucumber
Protein-first with near-zero glycemic impact. Keeps you full without disrupting blood sugar.
🧀
String cheese + celery
Quick, portable, satisfying. Fat content blunts any blood sugar response.

Foods to focus on
🥦
Non-starchy vegetables
Fill half the plate — broccoli, zucchini, spinach, peppers. High fiber slows glucose absorption.
🐟
Lean proteins
Salmon, chicken, turkey, legumes. Protein blunts blood sugar spikes and supports evening energy.
🫘
Beans & lentils
High fiber, high protein, low glycemic. Easy to add to soups, tacos, salads.
🫒
Healthy fats
Olive oil, avocado, nuts. Reduce the glucose spike from carbs eaten in the same meal.
🌾
Whole grains in small portions
Brown rice, quinoa, oats — right-sized. A ½ cup with protein and vegetables causes minimal blood sugar impact.

Low-impact, back-safe, and the most effective time for blood sugar management. Start here every day.

2 sets × 10 reps each side. Builds lumbar support without loading the spine. Marcus's #1 priority for your back.
🏊
Pool walking or swimming
2× per week when possible. Water removes spinal load — ideal for building cardio while your back heals.
🔄
Glute bridges
3 sets × 12 reps. Strengthens glutes and hamstrings that support your lower back. Do these before walks.
